# Feedback

Your feedback helps improve Cluster Terminal. Whether you have found a bug, want to request a feature, or have a suggestion for improvement, here is how to reach us.

## Reporting a Bug

A good bug report helps us reproduce and fix the issue quickly. Please include as much detail as possible.

### What to Include

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe the issue">
    Explain what happened. Be specific: "Chart crashes when adding RSI to a 1m BTCUSDT cluster chart" is much more useful than "chart broken."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Steps to reproduce">
    List the exact steps needed to trigger the issue:
    1. Open workspace X
    2. Switch to ETHUSDT 5m
    3. Add Absorption Alpha indicator
    4. Scroll back to January 15
    5. Observe: chart freezes for 10 seconds
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expected vs. actual behavior">
    - **Expected**: Chart scrolls smoothly with the indicator loaded.
    - **Actual**: Chart freezes for 10 seconds and then recovers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Include a screenshot or screen recording">
    A screenshot showing the problem is worth a thousand words. If the issue is intermittent or involves animation/behavior, a short screen recording is even better.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note your version and environment">
    Include:
    - Cluster Terminal version (visible in Settings or the title bar)
    - Windows version (e.g., Windows 11 23H2)
    - GPU model if the issue is visual
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Bug Report Template

Use this template when reporting a bug:

```
**Description**: [One-line summary of the issue]

**Steps to reproduce**:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

**Expected**: [What should happen]
**Actual**: [What actually happens]

**Version**: [e.g., v1.2.0]
**OS**: [e.g., Windows 11]
**GPU**: [e.g., NVIDIA RTX 3060]

**Screenshot/Recording**: [Attach or link]
```

<Tip>
  The more detail you provide, the faster we can reproduce and fix the issue. Vague reports like "it does not work" often require multiple rounds of back-and-forth to diagnose.
</Tip>

## Requesting a Feature

Feature requests help us prioritize what to build next. When suggesting a feature:

1. **Describe the problem** you are trying to solve, not just the solution. "I want to compare two symbols side by side on the same price scale" is more useful than "add overlay mode."
2. **Explain your use case** -- how would this feature fit into your trading workflow?
3. **Provide examples** if possible -- screenshots from other platforms, sketches, or detailed descriptions of the desired behavior.
4. **Mention priority** -- is this a nice-to-have or a blocker for your workflow?

<Note>
  We read every feature request. Popular requests that align with the product roadmap get prioritized. Even if a request is not implemented immediately, it informs future design decisions.
</Note>

## What Happens After You Report

1. **Acknowledgment**: We confirm receipt of your report (typically within 24 hours on Telegram).
2. **Triage**: The issue is categorized by severity and type (bug, feature, improvement).
3. **Investigation**: For bugs, we attempt to reproduce the issue.
4. **Resolution**: Once fixed or implemented, the change ships in the next update. We will notify you if you reported via Telegram.
